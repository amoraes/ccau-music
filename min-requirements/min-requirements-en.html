Minimum requirements for Orchestral Exams

Orchestral Exams

  • All exams evaluates the knowledge of the student in the following subjects: music theory, music reading and instrument technique;
  • There are 4 levels of exams and a member is only allowed to play its instrument in the orchestra after being approved in the corresponding exam:
    • Level 1 - Music Rehearsals (local only)
    • Level 2 - Youth and Children's Meetings (local only) and Music Rehearsals (regional)*
    • Level 3 - Holy Services (local only)
    • Level 4 - Holy Services anywhere (Official Musician)
  • The content of these exams are cumulative and every student must take the exams in this order (i.e. a student cannot take a level III exam directly);
  • The Local or Regional Orchestra Conductor are the ones responsible for deciding if a student is ready to undertake an exam, after a preliminary evaluation.

* For organists the level 2 exam also includes playing before the Holy Service (also known as Half-hour playing).
